# SECTION 8: STUDY SYSTEM AND NEUROSCIENCE

## 10-Min Stop Protocol (Most Important Technique)
- Study 10 minutes → STOP → close source → brain dump 2 minutes
- The struggle to remember IS the encoding — easy recall means weak encoding
- Active retrieval creates 50% better long-term retention vs re-reading
- 4 rounds per block = 40 minutes active study with 8 minutes retrieval practice
- NEVER look at notes during brain dump — the gap is the learning

## NSDR — Non-Sleep Deep Rest (After EVERY Study Block)
- Lie flat on back, earphones, Huberman NSDR YouTube audio, phone DND, eyes closed
- Do NOT think about what you studied — let hippocampus replay automatically
- 10-15 minutes adds 20-30% additional memory consolidation
- Skipping NSDR loses approximately one third of the block — non-negotiable
- 4 study blocks means 4 NSDR sessions minimum every day

## PRIME Sentence (Before Every Block)
- Write: "I am studying [subject] today because [personal specific reason]"
- Activates amygdala which signals hippocampus to flag information as worth keeping
- Makes study 40% stickier vs studying without personal meaning

## BDNF Protocol
- Evening basketball + run generates peak BDNF (Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor)
- BDNF seals ALL 4 afternoon study blocks into long-term memory
- Athletics comes AFTER study blocks — never before
- Never skip evening athletics on a study day

- Explain to a 10-year-old — if you cannot do it simply you do not understand it
